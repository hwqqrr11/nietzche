Nietzsche in 1873

15th of October, 1844: Friedrich Nietzsche was born.
1849: Friedrich's Father, a Lutheran pastor, passes away.
1950: Franziska, Friedrich's mother, moves the family to Naumburg.
1858 - 1864: Nietzsche attends the Schulpforta Secondary School.
1864: Friedrich studies classical philology at Bonn University.
1865: Nietzsche continues studies at Leipzig and accidentally discovers Schopenhauer's main work in a second-hand bookstore.
1868: Friedrich first meets Richard Wagner.
1869: Nietzsche works as a Professor Extraordinarius of classical philology at the University of Basel, Switzerland.
1870: Frriendrich gets promoted to a full Proffesor. As a Swiss subject, he volunteers as a medical orderly in the Franco-Prussian war and serves briefly with the Prussian forces. He then returns to Basel in October, with his health deteriorated.
1872: Nietzsche publishes his first book:Die Geburt der Tragödie aus dem Geist der Musik ("The Birth of Tragedy out of the Spirit of Music")
1873: Friedrich publishes of the first two Unzeitgemässe Betrachtungen ("Untimely Meditations"): David Strauss, der Bekenner und Schrisftsteller (“David Strauss, the Confessor and Writer”) and Vom Nutzen und Nachteil der Historie für das Leben (“On the Uses and Disadvantages of History for Life”).
1874: Nietzsche publishes Schopenhauer als Erzieher (“Schopenhauer as Educator”), the third Untimely Meditation.
1876: After many delays, Nietzsche completes and publishes “Richard Wagner in Bayreuth” as the last of the Untimely Meditations.
1878 Friedrich publishes the first part of Allzumenschliches, the Ein Buch für freie Geister ("Human, All-Too-Human: A Book for Free Spirits").
1879: Nietzshe resigns from the university of Basel and publishes Vermischte Meinungen und Sprüche ("Mixed Opinions and Maxims") as a sequel for Allzumenschliches ("Human, All-Too-Human").
1880: Friedrich publishes Der Wanderer und sein Schatten ("The Wanderer and His Shadow"") as a second and final sequel of Allzumenschliches ("Human, All-Too-Human").
1881: Nietzsche publishes Die Morgenröte ("The Dawn").
1882: Nietzsche publishes Die Fröhliche Wissenschaft ("The Gay Science").
1883-1885: Friedrich writes and publishes Also sprach Zarathustra: Ein Buch für Alle und Keinen ("Thus Spoke Zarathustra: A Book for All and None").
1886: Nietzsche publishes Jenseits von Gut und Böse ("Beyond Good and Evil").
1887: Nietzsche publishes Zur Genealogie der Moral ("Toward a Genealogy of Morals").
1888: Nietzsche was admited to an asylym and later released in care of his mother. During that time, he publishes:

Der Fall Wagner ("The Wagner Case"),

Götzen-Dämmerung ("Twilight of the Idols"),

Der Antichrist ("The Antichrist"),

Ecce homo: Wie man wird, was man ist ("Ecce Homo: How One Becomes What One Is"),

Nietzsche contra Wagner("Nietzsche contra Wagner; Out of the Files of a Psychologist").
1897: Nietzsche's mother dies. His sister moves him to Weimar.
25th of August, 1900: Friedrich Nietzsche dies at the age of 55.
1901: His sister publishes some of his notes under the title "Der Wille zur Macht" ("The Will to Power"). The scholarly consensus has been that it does not reflect Nietzsche's intent.
